Biography

Ken Jordan began his life in northern california and was an orphan at 4 months old. His early life was very tramatic as he was moved from orphanage to foster care up until the age of six. Ken was adopted by the Lebrun family at the age of 6 along with his younger brother Andrew. His adopted parents were very strict farmers who always wanted children, especially boys to help out on the farm. Ken was homeschooled through highschool and led a very sheltered life. By the age of 15, Ken had become an EMT/Firefighter / in nearest small town and began working for the DFPA as a wildland firefighter by the age of 17.

As soon as Ken became of age, he decided to get away from his oppressive parents and join the military. He found himself spending the next 16 years as a Radar Technician in the US Airforce, working and traveling all over the world. As fate would have it, he was sent to 2 different war zones and unfortunately was injured in an attack early on in his career. After struggling with these injuries for 16 years, Ken finally determined he should separate from the military and change his path.

After working for the telecommunication industry as a field operations technician for nearly 10 years, Ken decided he would go to school. He is currently working on a Bachelors in Electrical Engineering and wants to work in the space technology sector.
